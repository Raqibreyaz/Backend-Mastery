An invoice is sent before payment to ask the customer to pay, while a receipt is given after payment as proof that the money has been received.

Meaning
An invoice is a payment request for goods or services provided.

A receipt is a payment confirmation issued after the transaction is completed.

Timing
An invoice is generated before the customer pays.

A receipt is generated after the customer pays.

Purpose
The purpose of an invoice is to show the amount due and collect payment.

The purpose of a receipt is to show that the payment has already been made.

Contents
An invoice usually contains item details, total amount, due date, and payment terms.

A receipt usually contains amount paid, payment date, payment method, and item or service details.

Example
If a freelancer finishes a project and asks for payment, they send an invoice first.

After the client pays, the freelancer sends a receipt as proof of payment.
