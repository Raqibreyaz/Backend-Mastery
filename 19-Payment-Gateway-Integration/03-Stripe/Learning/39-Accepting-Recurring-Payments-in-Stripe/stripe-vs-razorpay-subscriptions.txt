Quick Reference:

Stripe subscriptions are created for a customer inside Stripe Billing, while Razorpay subscriptions are created by first defining a plan and then linking that plan to a customer subscription.

Stripe supports trials and moves a subscription from trialing to active when billing starts, and Razorpay also supports trial periods plus upfront charges in its subscription setup.

Stripe’s recurring flow is strongly tied to invoices and automatic charging of the saved payment method, while Razorpay emphasizes automated scheduled charges on the configured billing cycle.

Stripe offers richer billing controls such as proration, dunning management, and subscription schedules with multiple phases, while Razorpay focuses more on simpler plan management, pause/cancel flows, retries, and alerts.

Stripe is generally stronger for global SaaS and international recurring billing, while Razorpay is usually the better fit for India-first businesses and local payment behavior.

Stripe subscription statuses commonly include trialing, active, and past_due, while Razorpay highlights states and events around active subscriptions, failing payments, and halted subscriptions after retries are exhausted.

Both systems support webhook-based subscription updates, so backend systems should listen to provider events instead of trusting only the frontend.

A simple mental model is: Stripe subscription = customer-centric billing engine with invoices and phases, while Razorpay subscription = plan-centric recurring billing with strong dashboard-driven automation.
