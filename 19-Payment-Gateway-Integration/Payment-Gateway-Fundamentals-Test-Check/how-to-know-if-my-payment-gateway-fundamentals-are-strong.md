Here are 8 quick-reference points to judge whether your **payment gateway fundamentals** are strong:

- Strong fundamentals mean you understand the **full payment lifecycle**, not just how to integrate a button.
- You should be able to clearly explain concepts like **checkout flow, payment object, payment attempt, webhook, refund, retry, capture, and failure handling**.
- A good test is whether you can tell **which system part is the source of truth** at each stage, especially why backend webhook confirmation is safer than frontend success callbacks.
- You should be able to handle **edge cases** confidently, like duplicate attempts, expired sessions, failed authentication, retries, and delayed capture.
- To compare Stripe and Razorpay well, compare them **concept by concept**, not just feature by feature.
- Stripe is useful for learning deeper **payment abstractions** like checkout session, payment intent, charge, and state transitions.
- Razorpay is useful for learning **real India-first production flows**, such as orders, subscriptions, offers, billing cycles, and local payment methods.
- The best final test is this: if you can explain **Stripe in Razorpay terms and Razorpay in Stripe terms** without confusion, then your payment fundamentals are becoming strong.
